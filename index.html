Project Idea

 

My project idea is a smartphone application that scans an individual’s online identity and measures how vulnerable they are in the cyber world and in turn gives the individual a rating score. Things such as how much personal information is readily available, how secure their accounts are ranging to the security settings on their social media accounts would be scored. My application would be the independent eye that gives individuals the guidance that is needed in today’s cyber focused world. Popular services such as Google gather information on an individual every time that they search the internet, this allows them to make an accurate picture of you and then they can tailor which advertisements to show you (Smith, 2014). My aim is to highlight how they may be open to this, so that the individual is able to make informed choices every day.

 

In this day and age, the amount of information that can be found on an individual online is astounding. My application would be an easy to use place that everyday people with no technical background could input a few key details and in turn let the application come up with its results. The way that the internet is used has changed massively in a relatively short period of time “it was a static network designed to shuttle a small freight of bytes or a short message between two terminals; it was a repository of information where content was published and maintained only by expert coders” (Dentzel, 2013). Oh how times have certainly changed!

 

My reason for creating an application is that I think everyone, no matter their awareness or skill level have a right to know how exposed they are online. The internet is a great tool but sharing too much of your personal information can be detrimental in the long run. My aim is to make as many people that I can aware of the dangers that they may face online. “68 percent of people believe they're doing all they can to protect themselves against cyberattacks”, (Palmer, 2019) – but is this really the case? People are not aware of what is happening in the background of their browser – I aim to point out what the internet knows about them. My proposed application would periodically remind people that their online presence is important – it will prompt them to make regular checks of their online identity. My proposed application would be the useful 3rd party that is there to help guide the unsuspecting through what can be a dangerous place. When an individual signs up for my proposed application, they will be asked to disclose how much they think they are sharing online and how far their security goes. They will be asked what services they are signed up to, this will be from a list of common online services such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and Google just to name a few.

 

The application will use this as a starting point and scan these services for the individuals account. From there the application will be able to begin to formulate what kind of an online presence the subscriber has, and scan their accounts for the level of security that they have enabled. For instance, the application will see what kind of information is readily available on their Facebook account, are their photos hidden? Are they able to send a friend request? It is based off of this readily available information that my application will rate the subscriber. The application service will try passwords to try to brute force and crack open the users’ accounts – all of this information will form part of the report that will be generated for the subscriber.

 

Dependant on the findings, my application would be designed to give the end user recommendations on fixing the issues that have been found. These will include guides to creating a strong password through to making the most of security settings on social media accounts.

 

This will be a mobile application but will also be available as a website. The application will be accessible on all mobile platforms such as iOS and android. Therefore, the main tool used to access this application/software will be a smartphone, followed by any web capable device such as a laptop or desktop PC. The end user will be able to sign up and have an account that they are able to access. Their historical scan data will be available for them to access as well so that they are able to see their progress. I would design the application to send reminders to the end user when a profile can may be needed.

 

I believe my project may be very complex to formulate. The code will have to tabulate an individual’s online presence – depending on how this is designed it could turn out to be quite complex. In my opinion I think my project idea is quite feasible, but at the moment I lack the skills necessary to complete it, although an experienced web developer/programmer should be able to design the application. My project will not require any specialised hardware, it will be able to run on any mobile device and likewise it will be able to run on the web interface of any computer.

 

If my project is successful it will outline to any individual how exposed they are on the internet. My project will take a snapshot of an individual’s online presence, whether that be social media all the way to online banking. My project will process the information into an easy to read and digest piece of writing. The aim is to make the information readily available and as transparent as possible – let’s face it, you should not have to be a tech wizard to know how exposed you are on the internet and it should be your right to know!
